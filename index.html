Chapter 5: Digital Citizenship and Responsible Technology Use

In today's interconnected world, understanding how to navigate the internet safely and responsibly is a crucial life skill. Digital citizenship encompasses not only technical proficiency but also ethical awareness, information literacy, and an understanding of how our online actions affect others in our communities and beyond.

Online safety involves several key principles: protecting personal information, understanding privacy settings, recognizing and avoiding phishing attempts, and maintaining healthy boundaries between screen time and offline activities. Students should be aware that their digital footprint is permanent and can have lasting consequences on their academic and professional futures.

Information literacy — the ability to find, evaluate, and use information effectively — is equally important. With the rise of misinformation, developing critical thinking skills to assess source credibility, check facts across multiple reputable sources, and understand the difference between primary and secondary sources has never been more essential in today's digital landscape.

Cyberbullying, digital privacy, and intellectual property rights are additional areas where students need education and guidance. Understanding copyright law, fair use, Creative Commons licenses, and academic integrity policies helps create responsible creators and consumers of digital content.

Discussion Questions: (1) How do social media algorithms affect the news and content you see daily? (2) What steps can you take to verify information before sharing it online? (3) How does your digital footprint affect future opportunities?
